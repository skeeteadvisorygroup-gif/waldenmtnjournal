Costco Shoppers React to Stricter Membership Enforcement

By Staff Reporter •

Some Costco shoppers are reporting stricter membership checks at store entrances and self-checkout areas as the warehouse retailer expands efforts to reduce membership sharing and tighten account verification procedures.

Customers in several regions say employees are now asking to see membership cards more consistently before shoppers enter stores and, in some cases, verifying that the photo attached to the account matches the person using it at checkout. Reports of additional checks at self-checkout stations have also increased, with some shoppers saying employees are scanning membership cards or requesting identification when account ownership appears unclear.

Costco has publicly stated that the company is increasing enforcement of existing membership rules rather than introducing a completely new policy. The retailer has long prohibited membership sharing outside authorized household cardholders, and membership cards include customer photographs for verification purposes.

The enforcement push became more visible after Costco executives discussed the issue during company earnings calls and investor updates. According to Reuters, Costco began deploying membership card scanners at store entrances as part of a broader effort to crack down on unauthorized membership use and improve verification procedures. Reuters reported that the company had been testing stricter entry systems at select locations before expanding the approach more broadly.

Costco executives have also linked some of the changes to increased misuse at self-checkout areas, where employees may have less direct interaction with customers during transactions. During prior earnings commentary, Costco Chief Financial Officer Richard Galanti said the company was seeing "significant" growth in membership-sharing behavior and planned to strengthen verification measures accordingly.

The company's official membership policy states that memberships are non-transferable and may only be used by active members and their authorized household cardholders. Costco's website also notes that members may be asked to show identification to confirm account ownership during shopping visits or checkout transactions.

Shoppers are noticing the changes most frequently at self-checkout areas, where retailers across the industry have increased oversight in recent years. Some Costco customers report longer interactions during checkout if employees pause transactions to verify membership details, while others say the process has had little visible effect on their visits.

The stricter enforcement does not appear to be fully standardized nationwide, and Costco has not announced that identical procedures are being implemented at every location. Reports suggest enforcement may vary depending on store traffic, staffing levels, and local operational decisions.

Retail analysts say Costco's membership-based business model gives the company a strong incentive to protect membership integrity. Membership fees account for a substantial portion of Costco's operating income, making unauthorized account sharing a larger concern than it might be for traditional retailers.

For shoppers using their own active membership cards, the practical impact may remain limited. However, customers who previously relied on borrowed cards or shared memberships could encounter additional screening or denied transactions if account ownership cannot be verified.

Costco shoppers visiting stores in the coming months may increasingly notice entrance scanners, additional employee checks, or more active oversight at self-checkout areas as enforcement efforts continue expanding. The company has not provided a nationwide timeline for broader rollout plans, but executives have indicated that membership verification will remain an ongoing focus.
